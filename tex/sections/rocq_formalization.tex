\section*{Rocq Formalization}

Most of our results are formalized in Rocq~\cite{Rocq}, see~\cite{OurRocqRepo}.
We build on the Coquand--Siles~\cite{CoquandRegexEquality,RocqRegexpBrzozowski}
formalization of regular-expression equivalence via Brzozowski derivatives.
The finiteness result is important because all notions of finiteness are not
equivalent in a constructive setting. In the Rocq development we are working
toward a Kuratowski-finiteness result using normalized representatives of
regular expressions, so that repeated derivatives can be checked to generate
only finitely many distinct states using the equivalence procedure
in~\cite{CoquandRegexEquality}. This should be a slight improvement, since the
result in~\cite{CoquandRegexEquality} is based on a weaker constructive notion
of finiteness.
